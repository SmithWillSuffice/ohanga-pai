UBInterested

Published on 2023-07-06

Contents

Recent “friendlies” were hosting UBI shills, stabbing us in the back and twisting the knife, so I had to write to defend MMT.

I am so bitter about this I cannot name names, because that would imply friendly banter. I have to attack the ideas.

When I name names, like Jason Hickel, or Michael Hudson, or Paul Krugman, or Richard Wolff, and have something against their ideas to say, I’m always doing it in a good faith friendly way. That’s because I trust (some of) these people. That is, I trust they are writing or speaking in good faith. Sometimes I see their ideas are born out of ignorance, and so results in straw-manning MMT, but I can usually see where the fault lies, and so will not shy from calling hem out. I know full well I could be misinterpreting them, or might even have things wrong myself on the facts.

However, UBI and crypto-noncurrency are different stories. These are things that act directly against working class interests, and in the most insidious ways — since it is not hard to “sell” a good decent honest working class pleb on these ideas. Who doesn’t want the get rich quick (crypto) or have free money (UBI)?
