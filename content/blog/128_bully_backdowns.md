## The MMT Backdowns

Yet another way MMT becomes much more than accounting & ledgers is the role 
the currency plays in such peace & reconciliation.

This is because there is always rebuilding --- the opposite of the WW-I fake 
peace.

Why did the USA not become "bankrupt" in sponsoring the Marshall Plan and 
helping Japan rebuild after WW-II?

It is because the US Congress cannot ever run out of their own scorepoints.

Yeah, but why was the shipping of material capital to Europe and Japan 
post-war not causing inflation? Why did the i𝐧f𝐥a𝐭o𝐧 𝐛o𝐨g𝐞y𝐦a𝐧 stay asleep?

The answer is because paying workers for producing things is never inflationary. 
Unless you decide to pay a higher than market wage. Which you can always do 
to attract more public sector workers. It is a policy preference. Do you prefer 
an upwards adjustment in the price level, or unemployment, or at least less 
public sector employment?

In any case, this is not inflationary. Pulling more workers out of the 
private sector into the public sector is inflation neutral if there is a 
tax offset imposed on the specific sector the workers are desired to be 
drawn from. If they were already unemployed then there is no inflation 
pressure and so no need for a tax offset. The Job Guarantee policy can always 
simply rely on the tax bracket creep and other automatic stabilizers which 
always automatically increase tax return in response to higher government 
net spending.  Any frictional residual inflation is some other bottleneck 
not considered, and is normally nothing to worry about (let's be generous 
and presume the country has low corruption levels).

## New Zealand Fully Funds the United Nations

At a governmental level then, which means also at the United Nations level 
(basket of currencies, or SDR's) there is never an issue with "finding the 
money."  The issue in rebuilding after war and conflict is finding the real 
capital resources and labour.  Don't you kinda' wish the people running the UN 
knew this? And so never worried about "getting the USD scoreponts"? I sure do. 

To make the point with an extreme case:
* The UN does not need the USD, and never has.
* The UN could operate entirely with a "puny currency" like the NZD. Our 
government can just fully fund the UN, alone. (Extreme case ok, to make the 
point).
* The consequence could be some pass-through inflation into the NZ domestic 
economy, or a need for NZ to apply capital constraints, to prevent foreigners 
who now hold NZD scorepoints with our RBNZ or other G7 banks, and soon 
BRICS+ international clearing banks.
* NZ can always accommodate any such price pressure --- sometimes mistakenly 
called "inflation" for the scare value --- because domestic wage adjustments 
can _always be made_ without question. It is only NZ government willingness 
to do so, not lack of capacity to do so.

The threat to New Zealand politics from fully funding the United Nations is 
thus purely psychological: people tend to prefer the horrors of unemployment 
to the fake horror of inflation, because the bourgeois middle and upper classes 
never really "see" the effects of unemployment. Folks, the horror of seeing 
price tags increase in numerical ordination is somehow more horrific than 
ruining someone's life through involuntary unemployment???

The only real social cost to NZ of fully funding the United Nations operations 
would be the annoyance of the possibly frequent domestic price adjustment. It 
is annoying. But that is all it is --- for a well-educated population (which 
has to include government, _60% of us understand MMT including 60% of members of parliament_ is probably the bare minimum).

This tells you NZ would not _really_ be funding the UN!

The UN would be using the NZD scorepoints to make payments. But the real
